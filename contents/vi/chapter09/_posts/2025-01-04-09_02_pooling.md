## Mục tiêu Học tập

Sau khi hoàn thành bài học này, bạn sẽ hiểu sâu về **Eight Schools problem** - một trong những ví dụ kinh điển và được trích dẫn nhiều nhất trong Bayesian hierarchical modeling. Bạn sẽ implement và so sánh chi tiết ba pooling strategies, visualize shrinkage effect, và hiểu khi nào partial pooling thực sự có lợi. Quan trọng hơn, bạn sẽ thấy hierarchical models không chỉ là lý thuyết, mà là công cụ thực tế để đối phó với uncertainty và small sample sizes.

## Giới thiệu: The Eight Schools Study

### Bối cảnh Lịch sử

Vào những năm 1980, một nghiên cứu giáo dục lớn được thực hiện để đánh giá hiệu quả của **SAT coaching programs** (các chương trình ôn thi SAT). Nghiên cứu này, được Donald Rubin công bố năm 1981, đã trở thành một trong những ví dụ kinh điển nhất trong thống kê Bayesian.

**Thiết kế nghiên cứu**:
- **8 trường trung học** tham gia
- Mỗi trường thực hiện một **randomized experiment**:
  - Treatment group: Học sinh tham gia coaching program
  - Control group: Học sinh không tham gia
- Measure: Điểm SAT sau chương trình

**Kết quả thu được**:
- Mỗi trường estimate được **treatment effect** $$y_j$$ (difference between treatment and control)
- Mỗi estimate có **standard error** $$\sigma_j$$ (từ within-school analysis)

### Dữ liệu Eight Schools

```python
import numpy as np
```

## 1. Strategy 1: Complete Pooling - "All Schools Are The Same"

**Assumption**: Tất cả schools have **identical effect**. Ignore school identity.

**Model**:
$$
y_j \sim \text{Normal}(\mu, \sigma_j)
$$

Chỉ có một parameter $$\mu$$ (common effect), treat $$\sigma_j$$ as known.

### 1.1. Implementation

```python
# Complete pooling: Weighted average (inverse-variance weighting)
# More precise estimates (smaller σ) get more weight
weights = 1 / sigma**2
pooled_mean = np.average(y, weights=weights)
pooled_se = 1 / np.sqrt(np.sum(weights))

print("\n" + "=" * 70)
print("STRATEGY 1: COMPLETE POOLING")
print("=" * 70)
print(f"\nPooled estimate: {pooled_mean:.2f} ± {pooled_se:.2f}")
print(f"95% CI: [{pooled_mean - 1.96*pooled_se:.2f}, " +
      f"{pooled_mean + 1.96*pooled_se:.2f}]")
print(f"\n→ All schools get the SAME estimate: {pooled_mean:.2f}")
print(f"→ Ignores between-school variability")
print(f"→ Assumes differences are pure noise")
print("\n❌ PROBLEMS:")
print("   • Underestimates true heterogeneity")
print("   • Doesn't answer: 'Which school is best?'")
print("   • Overly confident (SE too small)")
print("=" * 70)
```

**Interpretation**:
- Pooled estimate ≈ 7.7 SAT points
- **All schools** treated as having effect = 7.7
- School A (observed 28) và School C (observed -3) both get 7.7!

### 1.2. Bayesian Complete Pooling

```python
# Bayesian version
with pm.Model() as complete_pooling:
    # Single common effect
    mu = pm.Normal('mu', mu=0, sigma=10)
    
    # Likelihood (treat sigma as known)
    y_obs = pm.Normal('y_obs', mu=mu, sigma=sigma, observed=y)
    
    # Sample
    trace_complete = pm.sample(2000, tune=1000, chains=4, random_seed=42,
                               return_inferencedata=True, progressbar=False)

# Extract
mu_complete = trace_complete.posterior['mu'].values.flatten()

print("\n" + "=" * 70)
print("BAYESIAN COMPLETE POOLING")
print("=" * 70)
print(f"\nPosterior for μ:")
print(f"   Mean: {mu_complete.mean():.2f}")
print(f"   SD: {mu_complete.std():.2f}")
print(f"   89% HDI: {az.hdi(mu_complete, hdi_prob=0.89)}")
print("=" * 70)
```

## 2. Strategy 2: No Pooling - "All Schools Are Completely Different"

**Assumption**: Schools are **completely independent**. No information sharing.

**Model**:
$$
y_j \sim \text{Normal}(\theta_j, \sigma_j)
$$

Mỗi school có own effect $$\theta_j$$, no connection between schools.

### 2.1. Implementation

```python
# No pooling: Use observed data directly
# Best estimate for θ_j is simply y_j (the observed effect)
no_pool_means = y.copy()
no_pool_ses = sigma.copy()

print("\n" + "=" * 70)
print("STRATEGY 2: NO POOLING")
print("=" * 70)
print("\nIndividual estimates (mean ± SE):")
print("-" * 70)
print(f"{'School':<8} {'Effect':<10} {'SE':<10} {'95% CI':<20}")
print("-" * 70)
for i in range(J):
    ci_low = no_pool_means[i] - 1.96 * no_pool_ses[i]
    ci_high = no_pool_means[i] + 1.96 * no_pool_ses[i]
    print(f"{schools[i]:<8} {no_pool_means[i]:<10.2f} {no_pool_ses[i]:<10.2f} " +
          f"[{ci_low:.1f}, {ci_high:.1f}]")

print("\n❌ PROBLEMS:")
print("   • High uncertainty for ALL schools")
print("   • No 'borrowing of strength'")
print("   • School C: Effect = -3 ± 16 (CI includes -35 to +29!)")
print("   • Can't distinguish signal from noise")
print("=" * 70)
```

**Interpretation**:
- School A: 28 ± 15 → CI = [-2, 58]
- School C: -3 ± 16 → CI = [-35, 29]
- **Huge uncertainty**, especially for schools with large $$\sigma_j$$

### 2.2. Bayesian No Pooling

```python
# Bayesian version
with pm.Model() as no_pooling:
    # Separate effect for each school (independent priors)
    theta = pm.Normal('theta', mu=0, sigma=10, shape=J)
    
    # Likelihood
    y_obs = pm.Normal('y_obs', mu=theta, sigma=sigma, observed=y)
    
    # Sample
    trace_no_pool = pm.sample(2000, tune=1000, chains=4, random_seed=42,
                              return_inferencedata=True, progressbar=False)

# Extract
theta_no_pool = trace_no_pool.posterior['theta'].values.reshape(-1, J)
theta_no_pool_means = theta_no_pool.mean(axis=0)
theta_no_pool_stds = theta_no_pool.std(axis=0)

print("\n" + "=" * 70)
print("BAYESIAN NO POOLING")
print("=" * 70)
print("\nPosterior estimates:")
for i in range(J):
    print(f"  School {schools[i]}: {theta_no_pool_means[i]:.2f} ± " +
          f"{theta_no_pool_stds[i]:.2f}")
print("\n→ Similar to observed data (prior is weak)")
print("=" * 70)
```

## 3. Strategy 3: Partial Pooling (Hierarchical Model) - "Schools Are Similar But Not Identical"

**Assumption**: Schools differ, but they're **related** (all from same population).

**Hierarchical model**:
$$
\begin{align}
y_j &\sim \text{Normal}(\theta_j, \sigma_j) && \text{(Likelihood)} \\
\theta_j &\sim \text{Normal}(\mu, \tau) && \text{(School effects)} \\
\mu &\sim \text{Normal}(0, 10) && \text{(Population mean)} \\
\tau &\sim \text{HalfNormal}(10) && \text{(Between-school SD)}
\end{align}
$$

**Key insight**: $$\theta_j \sim \text{Normal}(\mu, \tau)$$ creates **information sharing**.

### 3.1. Implementation

```python
# Hierarchical model
with pm.Model() as hierarchical:
    # Hyperpriors (population-level)
    mu = pm.Normal('mu', mu=0, sigma=10)
    tau = pm.HalfNormal('tau', sigma=10)
    
    # School-level effects (connected via μ, τ)
    theta = pm.Normal('theta', mu=mu, sigma=tau, shape=J)
    
    # Likelihood (data level)
    y_obs = pm.Normal('y_obs', mu=theta, sigma=sigma, observed=y)
    
    # Sample
    trace_hier = pm.sample(2000, tune=1000, chains=4, random_seed=42,
                           return_inferencedata=True, 
                           target_accept=0.95)

# Diagnostics
print("\n" + "=" * 70)
print("MCMC DIAGNOSTICS")
print("=" * 70)
summary = az.summary(trace_hier, var_names=['mu', 'tau', 'theta'])
print(summary)
print("\n→ Check r_hat < 1.01 and ess_bulk > 400")
print("=" * 70)
```

### 3.2. Extract và Interpret Results

```python
# Extract posterior samples
theta_hier = trace_hier.posterior['theta'].values.reshape(-1, J)
theta_hier_means = theta_hier.mean(axis=0)
theta_hier_stds = theta_hier.std(axis=0)

mu_hier = trace_hier.posterior['mu'].values.flatten()
tau_hier = trace_hier.posterior['tau'].values.flatten()

print("\n" + "=" * 70)
print("STRATEGY 3: PARTIAL POOLING (Hierarchical)")
print("=" * 70)
print(f"\n📊 POPULATION-LEVEL PARAMETERS:")
print(f"   μ (average effect across schools): {mu_hier.mean():.2f} ± {mu_hier.std():.2f}")
print(f"   89% HDI: {az.hdi(mu_hier, hdi_prob=0.89)}")
print(f"\n   τ (between-school variability): {tau_hier.mean():.2f} ± {tau_hier.std():.2f}")
print(f"   89% HDI: {az.hdi(tau_hier, hdi_prob=0.89)}")

print(f"\n🏫 SCHOOL-LEVEL ESTIMATES:")
print("-" * 70)
print(f"{'School':<8} {'Observed':<12} {'No Pool':<12} {'Partial Pool':<15} {'Shrinkage':<10}")
print("-" * 70)
for i in range(J):
    shrinkage = abs(y[i] - theta_hier_means[i])
    print(f"{schools[i]:<8} {y[i]:<12.2f} {theta_no_pool_means[i]:<12.2f} " +
          f"{theta_hier_means[i]:<15.2f} {shrinkage:<10.2f}")

print("\n✅ KEY INSIGHTS:")
print("   • Extreme values (A: 28, C: -3) shrunk toward population mean")
print("   • Shrinkage proportional to uncertainty (σ)")
print("   • Reduced uncertainty compared to no pooling")
print("   • Balanced estimates!")
print("=" * 70)
```

## Tóm tắt

Eight Schools problem là ví dụ kinh điển cho hierarchical modeling:

**Ba strategies**:
1. **Complete pooling**: Ignores differences → underfits
2. **No pooling**: High uncertainty → no information sharing
3. **Partial pooling (Hierarchical)**: ✅ Best balance

**Shrinkage effect**:
- Extreme values pulled toward population mean
- Proportional to uncertainty ($$\sigma_j$$)
- Automatic regularization

**Key insights**:
- When data uncertain, hierarchical models borrow strength
- Population parameters ($$\mu, \tau$$) capture overall pattern
- School-specific effects ($$\theta_j$$) capture deviations
- Can predict for new schools

**When to use**:
- High uncertainty in individual estimates
- Small sample sizes for some groups
- Want to balance group-specific và population-level information

**Real-world applications**:
- Meta-analysis (combining studies)
- Clinical trials (multiple sites)
- Educational research (students in schools)
- A/B testing (multiple experiments)
