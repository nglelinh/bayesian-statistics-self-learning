Chapter 0 is the foundation for the rest of the course. Before moving into Bayesian inference, you need a shared language for uncertainty, data description, and model building in both mathematical and computational terms. This chapter therefore gathers the core ideas from probability, statistics, and practical data work that you will rely on throughout later chapters.

In this chapter, you will review common probability distributions, descriptive statistics, basic Python, linear algebra, and calculus at a level that is useful for modeling. The chapter then extends to essential concepts such as joint distributions, expectation, variance, sampling distributions, standard error, the law of large numbers, the central limit theorem, and topics that often cause confusion, including p-values, t-tests, log-probability, and basic model assumption checks.

The goal of Chapter 0 is not to turn you into a pure mathematician, but to give you enough intuition to read models meaningfully. By the end of the chapter, you should feel more comfortable interpreting formulas, following statistical arguments in later chapters, and using simulation as a tool for checking your own understanding.
